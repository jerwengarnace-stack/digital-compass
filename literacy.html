Building Digital Literacy

Develop the skills needed to use technology and the internet effectively, responsibly, and critically.

üë£ Understanding Your Digital Footprint

Your digital footprint is the unique trail of data you leave behind whenever you use the internet. This includes your social media activity, emails, browsing history, and online purchases.

Passive vs. Active Footprints:

  • Passive Footprint: Data collected without your explicit knowledge (e.g., IP address, geolocation).
  • Active Footprint: Data you deliberately share (e.g., posting photos, writing reviews, filling out online forms).

Managing Your Footprint:

Rule of Thumb: Before you post, ask yourself: Would I be comfortable with my future employer or a university admissions officer seeing this?

In the age of information, critical evaluation of sources is vital. Learn to spot bias, differentiate facts from opinions, and verify information from multiple reputable sources.

ü§ù Online Etiquette (Netiquette)

Netiquette refers to the rules of polite behavior when communicating over the Internet. It's about respecting other users' time, privacy, and opinions.

  • Be Patient: Don't expect instant responses.
  • Be Concise: Keep messages short and to the point.
  • Avoid SHOUTING: Using all capital letters is seen as aggressive.
