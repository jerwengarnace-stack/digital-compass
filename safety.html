Phishing is the fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information like usernames, passwords, and credit card details by disguising oneself as a trustworthy entity in an electronic communication.

How to Spot a Phishing Email:

  • Urgent or Threatening Language: The message demands immediate action or threatens consequences if you don't comply.
  • Suspicious Sender Address: The email address looks slightly off (e.g., paypal@securesrvc.com instead of service@paypal.com).
  • Generic Greetings: Instead of using your name, the greeting is generic (e.g., "Dear Customer").
  • Mismatched Links: Hover your mouse over a link (without clicking!) and check the URL shown in the bottom corner of your browser. If it doesn't match the text, it's a scam.

Strong passwords are the first line of defense. Use a unique, complex password for every account and consider using a password manager.

Tips for Strong Passwords:

  • Use a combination of upper- and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Aim for at least 12 characters.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) whenever possible.

üì± Device Security

Keeping your operating system and applications updated is the simplest and most crucial step in device security. Updates often contain patches for security vulnerabilities.

Essential Device Tips:

  • Always use a screen lock (PIN, pattern, or biometric) on your phone and computer.
  • Install reputable antivirus/antimalware software on your computer.
  • Only download apps from official app stores (Google Play, Apple App Store).
  • Regularly back up important data to a cloud service or external drive.
